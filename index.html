Analysis of supersonic two-stage ejector. [funded by World Bank through NPIU-TEQIP under CRS scheme. Amount 9.59 lakh]
Co-PIs: Dr. Anil Kumar (Assistant Prof. KNIT, Sultanpur), Prof. PMV Subbarao (IIT Delhi) and Dr. Gaurav Singhal (Scientist DRDO LASTEC Delhi)
A new era of the ejector begins with the modification of the single stage ejector into twostage ejector (TSE). Two-stage compression is another way to decrease throttling loss and improve system efficiency. In this case, the second entrained stream enters at the exit of the mixing section for better utilization of the redundant momentum of discharge flow at the exit of mixing of the first-stage ejector. The system usually comprising with one motive stream inlet and two induced fluid inlets, the second induced fluid can be accelerated by the combined (motive and first induced stream) flow of first-stage.

Title: Analysis of Supersonic Shock Free Ejectors.
This thesis investigates two novel ejector design theory based on 1D gas dynamic, to improve the performance. The theories for ejector design were identified; the Constant Rate of Momentum Change (CRMC) and Constant Rate of Kinetic Energy Change (CRKEC). The Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) tool was utilized to validate geometries calculated using 1D gas dynamic theories. An experimental test rig was built with the required measuring instrument for the purpose experimental investigation of the both ejector systems. Both the CRMC and CRKEC ejectors have tested separately for the same design operating conditions. The experimentally and CFD calculated results were compared to each other both the ejectors. The results showed that both the theories have the capability to model shockless ejectors with improved performance.
